## Post 1
**Date:** 7 hours ago (June 2026)
**Topic:** Pipeline math — quality over volume

Instead of more pipeline volume, focus on conversion between meetings.

Sales math example:
- 12-meeting enterprise sales cycle
- 90% conversion per meeting = 28.5% win rate
- 92% conversion per meeting = 36.7% win rate
- That 2% lift means you need 22% LESS pipeline for same revenue

Result: less pressure to scale volume, more room for thoughtful outbound.

Key inputs: ICP targeting, next step setting, meeting prep, multithreading, buyer enablement.

## Post 2
**Date:** 4 days ago (June 2026)
**Topic:** Deliverability troubleshooting checklist

When emails go to spam, check in this order:
1. Send test email from every domain — verify SPF, DKIM, DMARC pass
2. Run inbox placement test with non-cold copy
3. If missing from placement test — domain is already cooked
4. Shorten copy — spam filters flag copy faster than ever
5. Use split test variants instead of spintax
6. Warm up minimum 2 weeks, full month is better
7. Buy two sets of infrastructure per month — one sends, one warms, rotate monthly
8. Have backup domains ready at all times

## Post 3
**Date:** 3 days ago (June 2026)
**Topic:** CAC math for founders considering cold email scale

Turned away a founder from buying Lavender because their CAC math didn't support scaled email yet.

Key framework:
- CAC payback period matters more than immediate revenue
- Free trials extend payback period but can lower CAC if they double conversions
- At $90/month product: if CAC is $900 per paying customer, need 10 months post-trial to break even
- Better to test ads first ($45/trial vs $90) to shorten payback, then layer in email
- Cold email doesn't scale the same way ads do for low-ACV products
