## Post 1
**Date:** 5 days ago (June 2026)
**Topic:** Performance review systems

Problems with traditional reviews: yearly = recency bias, weekly = too tactical, no time to apply feedback.

Previous approach: one Google Doc per direct report, notes throughout the week. Became too much admin, stopped doing it.

Now building performance tracking into MonsterOps (business operating system) alongside business metrics — individual performance data captured continuously.

## Post 2
**Date:** 6 days ago (June 2026)
**Topic:** Accountability and leadership visibility

Business operating systems make missed commitments visible to everyone — welcomed by strong team members but exposes wrong seats faster.

Key tension: transparency makes leadership weaknesses visible too. Most companies avoid real visibility because it reveals how strong or weak leadership actually is.

## Post 3
**Date:** 1 week ago (June 2026)
**Topic:** AI and metrics tracking

Instead of limiting metrics to avoid noise, use AI to track hundreds of indicators with clear operating parameters. System stays quiet until something goes outside bounds.

"Don't track anything you wouldn't act on" — still true for prioritization. But AI doesn't have human limitations; more data = better business reading.
